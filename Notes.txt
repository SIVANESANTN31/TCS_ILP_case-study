Slide 2 – What is Integrity?
Definition
Integrity means doing the right thing even when nobody is watching.
It involves:
Honesty
Responsibility
Respect
Accountability
Keeping promises
Quote:
"Integrity is doing the right thing, even when no one is watching."
Slide 3 – Why is Integrity Important?
Integrity helps to:
Build trust among team members
Improve company reputation
Create a positive work environment
Reduce conflicts
Increase customer confidence
Example: A trustworthy employee is more likely to receive important responsibilities.
Slide 4 – Practical Tip 1: Be Honest
Always tell the truth.
Real-life example
You accidentally delete an important project file.
❌ Wrong: Hide the mistake and blame someone else.
✅ Right: Inform your manager immediately and help recover the file.
Lesson: Honesty builds trust.
Slide 5 – Practical Tip 2: Take Responsibility
Accept your mistakes instead of making excuses.
Real-life example
During your internship, you send an email with incorrect information.
Instead of blaming the system, you:
Admit the mistake
Send the corrected email
Apologize
Lesson: Responsible employees earn respect.
Slide 6 – Practical Tip 3: Be Punctual
Respect everyone's time.
Examples:
Arrive at work on time.
Attend meetings on time.
Complete tasks before deadlines.
Real-life example
Two employees have the same skills.
The one who always delivers work on time is trusted with bigger projects.
Slide 7 – Practical Tip 4: Respect Company Rules
Follow company policies.
Examples:
Don't misuse office resources.
Don't share confidential information.
Follow security guidelines.
Real-life example
An employee refuses to share customer data with an outsider because it is confidential.
This protects both the company and its customers.
Slide 8 – Practical Tip 5: Give Credit to Others
Recognize your teammates' contributions.
Real-life example
A team project succeeds.
Instead of saying, "I did everything,"
say,
"Our whole team worked hard."
Lesson: Integrity includes fairness.
Slide 9 – Practical Tip 6: Avoid Cheating and Plagiarism
Always do your own work.
Examples:
Don't copy code.
Don't fake reports.
Don't copy presentations.
Real-life example
Instead of copying another developer's project and claiming it as your own, learn from it and create your own solution.
Slide 10 – Practical Tip 7: Protect Confidential Information
Never share sensitive company data.
Examples:
Customer information
Employee details
Company documents
Passwords
Real-life example
A friend asks about customer information.
You politely refuse because it is confidential.
Slide 11 – Internship Example
Scenario
You are an intern.
Your mentor asks whether the assigned task is complete.
Actually, you finished only 70%.
❌ Wrong
"Yes, it's completed."
✅ Right
"I've completed 70%. I'm still testing the remaining part and expect to finish it by tomorrow."
Result
Your manager trusts you because you are honest.

Slide 13 – Summary
Practice integrity by:
Being honest

Why is Integrity Important to the Company?
Integrity is the foundation of a successful organization. When employees act with honesty and responsibility, the entire company benefits.
1. Builds Trust
Customers trust the company's products and services.
Employees trust each other, improving teamwork.
Example: If a customer receives honest information about a product, they are more likely to return and recommend the company.
2. Improves Company Reputation
Companies known for ethical behavior attract more customers and business partners.
A good reputation helps the company grow.
Example: Companies that are transparent and keep their promises earn long-term customer loyalty.
3. Increases Productivity
Honest communication reduces misunderstandings.
Employees spend more time solving problems than dealing with conflicts.
4. Reduces Legal and Financial Risks
Following company policies and laws helps avoid fines, lawsuits, and fraud.
Protecting confidential information prevents data breaches.
5. Creates a Positive Work Culture
Employees feel respected and valued.
A positive environment leads to better teamwork and higher job satisfaction.
6. Supports Long-Term Growth
Customers stay loyal.
Investors and partners have confidence in the company.
The company attracts talented employees.
Real-Life Example
Imagine an employee notices a mistake in a customer invoice.
Without integrity: The employee ignores the mistake, and the customer is overcharged. This can lead to complaints, loss of trust, and damage to the company's reputation.
With integrity: The employee reports the mistake immediately, corrects the invoice, and informs the customer. The customer appreciates the honesty, and the company strengthens its reputation.
Key Message: One employee's integrity can protect the company's reputation, retain customers, and contribute to long-term success.
Presentation Closing Line
"Integrity is not just about personal values. Every honest decision made by an employee helps build customer trust, protects the company's reputation, strengthens teamwork, and supports the company's long-term success. When every employee acts with integrity, the entire organization becomes stronger."
